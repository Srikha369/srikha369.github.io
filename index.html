The Unknotting Number Under Connected Sum and Mutation

Senior Thesis

A knot theory thesis examining a 2025 result that disproved a long-standing conjecture — that unknotting number is additive under connected sum (Wendt, 1937) — via a counterexample built from a torus knot and its mirror image. The accompanying code constructs a directed graph of "Gordian adjacency" between knots to explore the strategy behind the counterexample and the open mutation-invariance problem it leaves behind.

Calculating Spontaneous Otoacoustic Phase Coherence

Published

Research on nonlinear dynamics in the auditory system, done as a Systems and Signal Processing Research Intern at the Fields Institute for Research in Mathematical Sciences. Constructed a new "phase coherence" metric for acoustic signals to enable quantitative analysis of otoacoustic emissions, built and cross-validated coupled-oscillator models against large-scale raw signal datasets, and applied Volterra series analysis to characterize blackbox nonlinear system behavior — work that led to an invited technical lecture at York University.
